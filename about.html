About Katz ACE Hardware

Serving Glastonbury Since 1920 — Over 100 Years of Helpful Hardware Service

A Century of Service to the Glastonbury Community

Katz ACE Hardware isn't just a hardware store—it's a Glastonbury institution. For over 100 years, our family-owned business has been the trusted destination for generations of homeowners, contractors, and DIY enthusiasts throughout Connecticut. What began as a small neighborhood shop in 1920 has grown into one of the region's most respected hardware destinations, all while maintaining the personal service and community values that have defined us from the very beginning.

Our History: A Legacy Built on Trust

1920: The Beginning

[ Photo: Original founder(s) ]

The Katz family, circa 1920

In 1920, as America emerged from the shadow of World War I and entered the prosperous "Roaring Twenties," the Katz family opened a modest hardware shop on Main Street in Glastonbury, Connecticut. At a time when Glastonbury was a small farming community of just over 4,000 residents, the Katz family recognized the need for a reliable source of tools, hardware, and supplies for local farmers, tradespeople, and homeowners.

The original store was a simple operation—wooden floors, hand-lettered signs, and merchandise displayed in wooden bins. Nails were sold by the pound, measured on a brass scale. Rope was cut to length. Paint was mixed by hand. But what set Katz Hardware apart from the very beginning was the family's commitment to knowing every customer by name and providing honest, helpful advice.

[ Historic photo: Original 1920s storefront or interior ]

The original Katz Hardware store on Main Street, Glastonbury, 1920s

The 1930s: Persevering Through the Great Depression

The stock market crash of 1929 and the Great Depression that followed tested businesses across America, and Glastonbury was no exception. Many local businesses closed their doors during these difficult years, but Katz Hardware persevered. The family made the difficult decision to extend credit to neighbors who were struggling, keeping careful records in handwritten ledgers and trusting that their community would repay them when times improved—and they did.

During the Depression, the store adapted by focusing on essential repair supplies and affordable tools that helped families maintain their homes and farms during hard times. The philosophy was simple: help your neighbors through difficult times, and they'll remain loyal customers for life. This approach cemented Katz Hardware's reputation as a business that truly cared about its community.

Inside Katz Hardware during the 1930s

The 1940s: Supporting the War Effort

[ Photo: WWII era store or victory garden supplies ]

Supporting Glastonbury's Victory Gardens, 1943

When World War II called America's young men to service, members of the Katz family answered the call alongside their Glastonbury neighbors. Those who remained home kept the store running and supported the war effort in every way possible. Katz Hardware became a collection point for scrap metal drives, helped residents maintain their Victory Gardens by providing seeds and gardening supplies, and assisted families in making do with rationed materials.

After the war ended in 1945, returning veterans found a changed America—and a changed Glastonbury. The post-war housing boom brought new families to town, and Katz Hardware was there to help them build, repair, and improve their homes. The GI Bill enabled many veterans to purchase homes, and the store expanded its inventory to meet the growing demand for building materials and home improvement supplies.

The 1950s & 1960s: Growth and Expansion

The post-war economic boom transformed Glastonbury from a small farming community into a thriving suburban town. The population more than doubled during this period, and Katz Hardware grew to meet the needs of these new residents. The store expanded its footprint, added new product lines, and hired additional staff—many of whom would remain with the company for decades.

The 1950s saw the introduction of new power tools and labor-saving devices that revolutionized home improvement. Katz Hardware stayed ahead of the curve, stocking the latest innovations and training staff to demonstrate and explain these new technologies to curious customers. The store became known not just for selling products, but for teaching customers how to use them safely and effectively.

In 1954, Ace Hardware—which had been founded in Chicago in 1924 as a buying cooperative for independent hardware stores—officially adopted the "Ace Hardware" name. Recognizing the benefits of cooperative buying power while maintaining local ownership and control, Katz Hardware eventually joined forces with Ace, becoming part of a network that would grow to become the world's largest hardware cooperative.

The 1970s & 1980s: Adapting to Change

[ Photo: Second/third generation owners ]

The next generation takes the helm

The 1970s brought new challenges: oil crises, inflation, and increasing competition from big-box retailers. But Katz ACE Hardware—now proudly bearing the Ace name—adapted and thrived. The store invested heavily in energy-efficient products as homeowners sought to reduce their heating costs. Staff became experts in weatherization, insulation, and energy conservation.

A new generation of the Katz family took the reins during this era, bringing fresh ideas while honoring the traditions that had made the store successful. They computerized inventory systems, expanded the paint department with the addition of Benjamin Moore paints, and enhanced the lawn and garden section to serve Glastonbury's growing suburban population.

The 1980s saw the rise of national home improvement chains, but Katz ACE Hardware doubled down on what made it special: personal service, expert advice, and deep community ties. While big-box stores competed on price alone, Katz competed on knowledge and relationships.

The 1990s & 2000s: Strengthening Community Bonds

As Glastonbury celebrated its own rich history, Katz ACE Hardware became increasingly involved in community events and local organizations. The store sponsored Little League teams, supported the Glastonbury Apple Harvest Festival, and donated supplies for countless community improvement projects.

The turn of the millennium brought the internet and new ways of shopping, but Katz ACE Hardware recognized that some things can't be replaced by technology: the ability to hold a product in your hands, to ask questions of a knowledgeable staff member, and to get advice tailored to your specific project. The store embraced technology as a tool while never losing sight of the human connections that truly matter.

2010s to Today: A New Century of Service

Bob, Current Owner

In 2020, Katz ACE Hardware celebrated its 100th anniversary—a remarkable milestone that few businesses ever achieve. While the COVID-19 pandemic made large celebrations impossible, the community showed its appreciation in countless ways, from heartfelt messages to continued support during uncertain times.

Today, Katz ACE Hardware continues to evolve while staying true to its roots. The store has expanded its offerings to include premium brands like Weber and Big Green Egg grills, Traeger smokers, YETI products, and EGO power equipment. The paint department, featuring Benjamin Moore's exceptional line, remains one of the finest in the region. And new services—from key cutting to screen repair, propane filling to glass cutting—ensure that customers can get what they need without driving all over town.

Perhaps most importantly, the core values established in 1920 remain at the heart of everything we do: treat every customer like a neighbor, provide honest and helpful advice, and give back to the community that has supported us for over a century.

Our Mission

Our mission is simple: to be the most helpful hardware store on the planet. We believe in treating every customer like a neighbor, providing expert advice, and carrying the products you need to get the job done right. This isn't just a slogan—it's been our way of doing business since 1920.

What Sets Us Apart

  • 105+ Years of Experience: Over a century of hardware expertise, passed down through generations.
  • Local Ownership: We're not a faceless corporation—we're your neighbors, and we live and work right here in Glastonbury.
  • Personal Service: We take the time to understand your project and help you find exactly what you need.
  • Expert Advice: Our knowledgeable staff can help with everything from simple repairs to complex renovations.
  • Quality Products: We carry trusted brands like Benjamin Moore, Weber, Big Green Egg, Traeger, YETI, EGO, and Scotts.
  • Full-Service Solutions: From key cutting to paint matching, propane filling to glass cutting, screen repair to blade sharpening—we do it all.
  • Community Commitment: We've supported Glastonbury through good times and bad for over 100 years, and we're just getting started.
